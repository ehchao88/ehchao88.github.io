ABOUT ME

I am currently an undergraduate student at Boston University pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computer Science. Having always been surrounded by technology, I always had a curiosity of what powered everything behind it, which naturally led me to become interested in programming.
My first experience with anything programming related was in 6th grade when I figured out how to look at the code of my favorite game at the time: Minecraft. From there, I was hooked and was led down a path of classes and self teaching where I picked up skills in everything from MIT's Scratch to C.
Beyond programming, music has always been a big part of my life and having played instruments ranging from the violin to the trumpet, I started to form an identity around my musical tastes. Guitar was the only instrument that struck a chord ;) and it has become a refreshing hobby to maintain since I was 8 years old.

BU Hub

For the current freshman class, BU implemented a new system for their general education requirements which is called "The Hub". It revolves around taking courses within 6 "Essential Capacities". Many of the current freshman class find the requirements for The Hub unintuitive and have trouble deciding which classes to take in order to efficiently fulfill all the requirements. Our project aims to help these students and after they input their major and which AP credits they received, provide them with a personalized list of classes which they may take throughout their time at BU which will ensure that the Hub is fulfilled.
